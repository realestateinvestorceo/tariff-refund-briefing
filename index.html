The verdict

The verdict

The play: free enrollment → paid filing + broker affiliate network

Percentage fees are already being undercut. ClaimYourTariffs launched at $1,497 flat for full filing. Our edge is using free ACH enrollment as a lead magnet, then converting to paid filing services. The real value is in separating duty types and preparing CAPE declarations — not bank registration.

Lead magnet — Free ACH enrollment help

ACH registration itself takes about 30 minutes if records are current — it's not hard enough to charge for. But 92% of importers (~304,000) still haven't done it and 12,300+ approved refunds are stuck in "rejected" status. Offer free enrollment help as the door-opener: walk them through the ACE portal, link their bank account, verify their importer number. You earn their trust and their data — then upsell the real service.

The real service — Full CAPE filing ($997-$1,997)

This is where importers actually get stuck and where the money is. Export their entry data from ACE, separate IEEPA duties from Section 301/232 tariffs (many importers paid stacked tariffs on the same shipments and can't tell which charges are refundable), clean and validate the data, format the CAPE declaration, submit it, and monitor for CBP discrepancies through payment. Flat fee for straightforward filings. Small percentage (2-3%) only for importers with hundreds of entries or genuinely complex tariff stacking.

Complex cases — Referral to law firm

Finally-closed import transactions, formal dispute filings, anti-dumping overlaps — these need a law firm. Refer to Thompson Coburn or similar, take a referral fee ($500-$2,000 per case). This is the only place where percentage fees (15-20%) are still defensible because there's real legal work involved.

The broker channel

There are 11,500 licensed customs brokers. Many are overwhelmed or don't want to handle CAPE filings for their smaller clients. Offer them $200-$300 per referral — they send you their sub-$500K clients, you handle the filing, they keep their client relationship. This is the highest-leverage channel because brokers already have trust and data.

Why flat fees win

Once CAPE launches and importers see the process, anyone charging 10%+ on a straightforward filing will get undercut. ClaimYourTariffs is already at $1,497 for full filing — on a $150K refund, that's 1%. Compare to the 25-40% some firms are still advertising. The market will converge on flat fees fast. Get there first.

Why the range is wide

At flat fees of $997-$1,997 per filing, per-client revenue is much lower than percentage-based models. The range depends on how many importers you can reach in a 6-12 month window and how many convert from the free ACH enrollment to paid filing. The moderate scenario ($1.5M-$2.5M net) is realistic with an active affiliate network and broker channel. Law firm referral fees on complex cases add upside on top.

This is not another ERC

ERC had 3.6M eligible businesses, $50K–$500K per client, and a 3+ year window with subjective eligibility. This has 330K importers, $1K–$2K per client at flat fees, a 6–12 month window, and binary eligibility. It's a real business — but a fundamentally different scale. The upside is in volume and the transition to ongoing trade compliance advisory.

Affiliate model

Using the ERC affiliate network

JZ's affiliates are a real asset — but the pitch, the math, and the lead qualification all change.

The pitch changes completely

ERC pitch was: "Do you have employees? You probably qualify for $50K-$500K."

Tariff pitch is: "Do you know anyone who imports physical products from overseas? They're owed money by the government and 92% can't collect it yet."

ERC affiliates don't need to learn customs. They need to ask one question: "Do you import anything from overseas?" If yes, send us the lead. We handle everything else.

Competition

Who's already in the game

The competitive landscape has segmented quickly. Every tier is active.

Tier 1 — Elite law firms

Crowell & Moring, Sidley Austin, Skadden, Thompson Coburn

~200 lawsuits filed by Crowell alone in the trade court. Representing Fortune 500 importers. They charge 20% of the refund (they only get paid if you get paid). Thompson Coburn is the firm JZ's contact mentioned.

Tier 2 — Big Four & advisory firms

Baker Tilly, KPMG, PwC, BDO, CLA, Cherry Bekaert

Building custom dashboards, readiness checklists, and full-service refund recovery practices. Targeting companies with $100K+ in refunds owed.

Tier 3 — Customs brokers

~11,500 licensed brokers (Shapiro, A.N. Deringer, JAS Worldwide)

These are the companies that handle import paperwork for businesses. They already have the data and system access for their existing clients. The natural first call for most importers.

Tier 4 — Tech platforms

Flexport, Descartes CustomsInfo

Offering free tariff refund calculators to attract leads. Software-assisted recovery. Strong with tech-savvy importers.

Tier 5 — Refund recovery intermediaries

Tariff Recovery Group, Refund-Advance.com, McCune Law Group

The space we'd compete in. TRG offers audit + managed recovery + upfront cash for your refund claim. McCune charges 15–25% of recovery. Already running urgency-driven marketing.

Tier 6 — Financial players

Texas Capital Bank, Refund-Advance.com

Buying refund claims at 60–80 cents on the dollar — importers get cash now, these firms collect the full refund later. Requires capital but clear economics.

What they're actually saying and doing

TECH PLATFORM Flexport — the most aggressive mover

What they built: A free "Tariff Refund Calculator" that lets any importer upload their 2025 import report and get an instant estimate of their IEEPA refund. They also launched an AI-powered "Audit Your Customs Broker" tool that reviews past filings, finds mistakes, and identifies refund opportunities. Both tools are free and available to non-Flexport customers.

Their real strategy: The free calculator is a lead-generation machine. Importers upload their actual import data to use it — giving Flexport a complete picture of their import activity, tariff exposure, and current broker. It's a Trojan horse to win brokerage business, not just refund fees.

Threat level for us: High for tech-savvy importers. Low for mid-market importers who don't know what CBP's online systems are.

INTERMEDIARY Tariff Recovery Group — the closest competitor to our model

Who they are: Founded by Aharon Margolin, a small-business financing veteran — not a customs expert. This is the most directly comparable company to what JZ would build.

Their model: (1) Free audit of your import profile. (2) Managed CAPE filing. (3) Claim purchase — they buy your refund claim upfront for immediate cash.

What's notable: They're using timeline uncertainty as an urgency lever: "CAPE is still being built, and processing 53 million import transactions will face delays well beyond launch."

Threat level for us: Direct competitor. First-mover advantage. But small team, new company — JZ's track record would be a stronger trust signal.

LAW FIRM Quinn Emanuel — built a dedicated "Tariff Refund Task Force"

What they built: A full branded landing page with podcast episodes, webinar recordings, client alerts, and a clear intake flow. A senior partner is publicly saying businesses should "act now."

Threat level for us: Low — targeting large enterprises at elite rates. But their messaging is worth studying for how to create urgency.

BIG FOUR / ADVISORY Baker Tilly — the most polished advisory play

What they built: A dedicated "Tariff Refund & Recovery Services" practice with a custom dashboard. They published a "Readiness Checklist" and are running CFO-level webinars. They tie refunds into broader advisory — pricing, tax, M&A.

Threat level for us: Medium — targeting $100K+ refunds with brand credibility we'd lack. But their pricing excludes smaller importers we'd target.

What this tells us about positioning

Nobody is doing exactly what JZ would do — combining high-velocity outbound sales with trade refund processing. The gap is the lower mid-market importer ($10K–$500K refund) who needs someone to call them, explain the opportunity, walk them through bank registration with CBP, and handle the CAPE filing. Nobody is owning that segment with real sales firepower yet.
